Forgotten:
The feeling of immense pressure stopped long ago, and the earth below now seemed plastic; a cosmic joke, punchline impending.
As we penetrated the last skimpy layers of the troposphere, expanding red rings began to grow on the ground far below us, engulfing geography a 5 year old could get a star for correctly identifying.
Marissa and I watched with a clinical detachment.  
My daughter (progeny/hopes/future) was in the bathroom and missed the whole thing.  
Her wavering voice leeched into my ear and a small hard pit formed in my stomach: 
"Dad, where's the toilet paper?"
